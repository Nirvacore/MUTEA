## Hojicha (ほうじ茶 · โฮจิฉะ)

### Tea Database Entry

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Common name | Hojicha (ชาเขียวคั่ว) |
| Scientific name | Camellia sinensis var. sinensis |
| Origin | Kyoto, 1920s |
| Processing | Steam → Dry → **Roast at 150-200°C** |
| Base material | Bancha, sencha leaves, stems (kukicha) |
| Color | Brown-red (น้ำตาลแดง) |

### Compound Profile

| Compound | Level | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Caffeine | 7-15 mg/cup | Very low (vs matcha 60-70 mg) |
| EGCG | Low | Destroyed by roasting |
| L-Theanine | Low-moderate | Partially degraded by heat |
| Pyrazines | High | Created by Maillard reaction — defines toasty aroma |
| Chlorophyll | None | Destroyed by roasting |

### Flavor Profile

- **Taste:** Toasty, nutty, caramel, earthy, warm
- **Aroma:** Pyrazines from Maillard reaction (amino acids + sugars at 150-200°C)
- **Mouthfeel:** Light, smooth, no astringency
- **Best served:** Hot or iced, plain or with milk

### Scientific Evidence

**Maillard Reaction & Pyrazine Formation:**
During roasting at 150-200°C, amino acids (L-theanine, lysine, glutamic acid) react with sugars (glucose, fructose) to form pyrazines — the nutty, toasty aroma compounds that define roasted tea. Higher temperature = more pyrazines but less catechins/theanine.

- Yin et al., 2023, *LWT* (50 citations) — Pyrazine formation in roasted tea
- Yang et al., 2020, *Food Research International* (58 citations) — Maillard reaction compounds
- Yang et al., 2022 (49 citations) — Additional pyrazine pathway research

**EGCG-Nitrogen Interaction:**
EGCG present in green tea leaves modulates which Maillard compounds form during roasting. EGCG preserves furans and aldehydes while suppressing nitrogen-containing volatiles. This explains why different starting materials produce distinct hojicha flavors.

- Li et al., 2025, *Food Research International* — DOI: 10.1016/j.foodres.2025.117618

## Genmaicha (玄米茶 · เกนไมฉะ)

### Tea Database Entry

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Common name | Genmaicha (ชาเขียวข้าวกล้องคั่ว) |
| Scientific name | Camellia sinensis + Oryza sativa |
| Origin | Japan (historically "poor man's tea") |
| Processing | Bancha/sencha + roasted/popped brown rice |
| Variants | Matcha-iri Genmaicha (with added matcha powder) |
| Color | Golden yellow (เหลืองทอง) |

### Compound Profile

| Compound | Level | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Caffeine | 15-20 mg/cup | Low (rice dilutes tea content) |
| EGCG | Moderate | From the tea leaf portion |
| L-Theanine | Low-moderate | From the tea leaf portion |
| GABA | Present | From germinated brown rice |
| Dietary fiber | Present | From brown rice |

### Flavor Profile

- **Taste:** Nutty, toasty, popcorn-like, sweet, comforting
- **Aroma:** Roasted rice with green tea freshness
- **Mouthfeel:** Light, warming, easy to drink
- **Best served:** Hot, everyday drinking

### GABA Connection

GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) is the main inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain. It plays roles in relaxation, anti-insomnia, blood pressure reduction, anti-depression, and anti-inflammation. Germinated brown rice is a significant natural source of GABA.

- Hou et al., 2023, *Critical Reviews in Food Science and Nutrition* (124 citations) — GABA in germinated grains
- Jabeen et al., 2024 — GABA enrichment in rice
- Feng et al., 2024 — GABA bioavailability

### Mu Tea Fit

**Collection:** Mu Sun (Bright, everyday, warm)

**Why it works:**
- "Bright, energizing, comforting" = Mu Sun theme
- Warm, comforting = feels like a hug in a cup
- Cheapest in the Japanese tea family = highest accessibility
- Everyday tea = high recurring purchase rate
- Matcha-iri variant bridges matcha and genmaicha collections
- Familiar rice flavor appeals to Asian (Thai) palate
