## Key Compounds

### L-Theanine

- **Content:** 6-46 mg/g depending on shade duration and cultivar
- **Function:** Relaxation without drowsiness, alpha brain wave promotion
- **Synergy:** Works with caffeine for "calm alertness"
- **Critical finding:** Stress-reducing effect depends on molar ratio of (caffeine + EGCG) to (theanine + arginine) being < 2

### EGCG (Epigallocatechin gallate)

- **Content:** 30-130 mg/g
- **Function:** Primary antioxidant, anti-inflammatory
- **Note:** Higher in culinary grades (more sun exposure)

### Caffeine

- **Content:** 20-40 mg/g (~60-70 mg/cup)
- **Function:** Alertness, metabolism
- **Note:** Buffered by L-Theanine (no jitters vs coffee)

## Scientific Evidence

### RCT 1: L-Theanine stress reduction
- **Finding:** L-theanine (200mg/day) reduced stress and cortisol in adults under stress
- **Key insight:** Effect depends on amino acid ratio, not absolute L-theanine amount
- **Evidence level:** Strong (6 RCTs reviewed)

### RCT 2: Matcha cognitive function
- **Finding:** Matcha improved attention and processing speed vs placebo
- **Evidence level:** Moderate (smaller sample sizes)

### RCT 3: EGCG and metabolic markers
- **Finding:** Green tea catechins improved fasting glucose and lipid profiles
- **Evidence level:** Strong (multiple meta-analyses)
