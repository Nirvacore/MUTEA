## Executive Summary

## Supplier Profiles

### Tier 1: Direct Farm

#### Obubu Tea (おぶぶ茶苑)
- **Location:** Wazuka, Kyoto Prefecture
- **URL:** wholesale.obubutea.com
- **Type:** Small family farm, direct-to-wholesale
- **Products:** Matcha, sencha, hojicha, genmaicha
- **Pricing:** 18-58% cheaper than Yunomi on comparable items
- **MOQ:** Low (flexible for small businesses)
- **Shipping:** Ships from Kyoto directly
- **Story value:** High — family farm, can visit, photo content
- **Mu Tea fit:** Primary source for Phase 1

### Tier 2: Marketplace

#### Yunomi
- **URL:** yunomi.life
- **Type:** Online marketplace aggregating Japanese tea farms
- **Products:** 1,500+ products from 100+ farms
- **Wholesale:** Application required (in progress)
- **Advantage:** Variety, discovery, multiple cultivars in one order
- **Pricing:** Retail markup but wholesale discount available
- **Shipping:** Consolidated from Japan
- **Mu Tea fit:** Discovery + variety source, especially for sampling

### Tier 3: Heritage Brands

#### Marukyu Koyamaen (丸久小山園)
- **Location:** Uji, Kyoto (founded 1704)
- **Products:** Ultra-premium ceremonial matcha
- **Price:** ¥10,000-30,000/100g
- **Access:** Difficult, primarily B2B for established businesses
- **Mu Tea fit:** Future Phase 3-4, when brand credibility is established

#### Ippodo (一保堂)
- **Location:** Kyoto (founded 1717)
- **Products:** Full range ceremonial to everyday matcha
- **Price:** ¥1,000-8,000/tin
- **Access:** Retail available, wholesale for volume
- **Mu Tea fit:** Benchmark and inspiration, possible Phase 3 collaboration

#### Fukujuen (福寿園)
- **Location:** Uji, Kyoto (founded 1790)
- **Products:** Matcha, gyokuro, sencha
- **Note:** Partners with Ito En for commercial products
- **Mu Tea fit:** Reference for quality standards

### Tier 4: OEM

#### Aiya (愛知)
- **Location:** Nishio, Aichi Prefecture
- **Type:** World's largest matcha manufacturer
- **Products:** Private label matcha, bulk supply
- **MOQ:** Typically 50-100kg minimum
- **Services:** Custom blending, packaging, certification (organic, kosher)
- **Mu Tea fit:** Phase 3 private label partner

## Shipping Logistics

| Method | Cost (2-3kg) | Time | Cold Chain | Best For |
|--------|-------------|------|------------|----------|
| EMS (Japan Post) | ¥3,000-5,000 | 5-7 days | Ice packs possible | Samples, small orders |
| DHL Express | ¥5,000-8,000 | 2-4 days | Temperature controlled | Urgent, premium |
| SAL (Japan Post) | ¥2,000-3,000 | 2-4 weeks | None | Non-perishable (hojicha, genmaicha) |
| Sea freight | Volume-based | 4-6 weeks | Container temp control | Phase 2+ bulk orders |

## Phased Sourcing Strategy

### Phase 1 (Now → 6 months): Discovery & Validation
- **Primary:** Obubu wholesale (direct, cheapest)
- **Secondary:** Yunomi wholesale (variety, sampling)
- **Volume:** 2-5kg/month
- **Goal:** Find the 5 SKUs that resonate

### Phase 2 (6-12 months): Subscription & Growth
- **Primary:** Obubu + Yunomi wholesale
- **Secondary:** Begin Tier 3 conversations
- **Volume:** 10-30kg/month
- **Goal:** Stable supply for subscription boxes

### Phase 3 (12-24 months): Private Label
- **Primary:** Aiya OEM for Mu Tea branded matcha
- **Secondary:** Direct farm for specialty/limited editions
- **Volume:** 50-100kg/month
- **Goal:** Own-brand products with custom blends
